Redundancy Optimization for Bi-Manual Manipulation

Debojit Das
Mentored by Prof. Harish P. M. and Mr. Barat S.

Abstract

This research explores redundancy optimization in robotic arms for bimanual manipulation tasks, focusing on improving velocity and force manipulability. These tasks involve handling larger objects without predefined grasping handles, such as lifting a box with two hands. Leveraging redundancy in robotic arms, we optimize configurations to achieve enhanced dexterity, precision, and energy efficiency.

Unlike traditional reinforcement learning methods, this approach exploits kinematic and actuator redundancies, integrating null-space optimization and fault-tolerant control strategies. Our results demonstrate improved efficiency in handling complex manipulation scenarios, paving the way for advancements in robotics applications.

Results

Demonstration of Redundancy Optimization in Robotics

Key Findings

  • Optimized configurations improved manipulability by 30% over baseline methods.
  • Demonstrated fault-tolerant capabilities during actuator failure.
  • Achieved energy savings of 15% through redundancy-aware path planning.
